Chicago, a vibrant city in Illinois, graces the eastern shore of Lake Michigan, leaving an indelible mark on all who visit. During my summer internship, I succumbed to its undeniable allure, finding myself drawn back time and again.At the heart of this metropolis lies Millennium Park, a captivating fusion of urban development and natural beauty. Nestled between the sparkling shores of Lake Michigan and the vast expanse of a lush green lawn, the park's iconic Cloud Gate sculpture, lovingly referred to as "The Bean," mesmerized me with its curvaceous reflection of the city's skyline. Amidst the bustling surroundings, the park creates an oasis of tranquility, a sanctuary to escape the city's hustle and bustle. My favorite activity was walking along the enchanting Chicago River, commencing my journey from the iconic Navy Pier. As I followed the meandering waterway, I found myself encircled by awe-inspiring skyscrapers harmoniously blending modern architecture with the soothing touch of nature. In the end, stands Willis Tower. Standing at this elevated vantage point of Skydeck, my breath was taken away by the mesmerizing skyline before me. The city's grandeur was unveiled, its towering buildings forming a breathtaking tapestry against the horizon.

California

North California boasts a seamless fusion of majestic mountains and serene seas, forming a landscape of unrivaled perfection. Starting the northern regions, my eyes were instantly captivated by the iconic silhouette of a dormant volcano, Mount Shasta. Its cone-like form, adorned with a pristine coat of snow, resembled a breathtaking painting come to life, leaving me in awe of nature's artistic prowess.To the east, the enchanting Lassen Volcanic National Park beckoned, a showcase of Earth's dynamic forces. Here, steaming fumaroles, boiling springs, and mesmerizing geothermal features presented a spectacle of the planet's raw power in motion, evoking a sense of humility and wonder within me.As I ventured south, a profound tranquility enveloped me in the Redwood Forests, where towering giants stood in silent reverence. Their ethereal presence created an otherworldly atmosphere, transporting me to a realm of imagination and reflection, deeply connected to the essence of nature.Lake Tahoe, a true gem of the region, awaited me with its crystalline waters nestled amidst the majestic Sierra Nevada mountains. From the vantage point of Cave Rock, I beheld the vast expanse of the lake, and a profound sense of peace washed over me, forging an unbreakable bond with nature.

Bay Area and Silicon Valley stand as iconic symbols of technological advancement in the United States. In contrast, Sacramento, the capital of California, exudes a rich historical charm through its old town and capitol house. San Francisco, known for its picturesque bridges and blossoming flowers, left a lasting impression on me as I drove through the majestic Golden Gate Bridge, the Bay Bridge, showcasing the nation's manufacturing capabilities. Amidst this urban wonder, landmarks like Lombard Street, the Palace of Fine Arts, and Golden Gate Park serve as poignant reminders of life's beauty and tranquility. Journeying south, San Jose emerges as a powerhouse in the southern Bay Area, housing the world's leading technology firms and bold startups. As an engineering background visitor, I found a shared camaraderie here, fostering a common voice among like-minded individuals. Venturing even further south, the enchanting Monterey Bay and the rugged beauty of Big Sur unfold, interconnected by the renowned California Highway 1. The unparalleled west coast views here, with their steep cliffs and the roaring Pacific Ocean, leave an indelible mark on my soul.

Continuing south along California One, we arrive at San Monica, boasting the liveliest beach on the coast. Unlike Monterey Bay, the ocean waves here roar with intensity, creating a captivating symphony. Despite the force, the beach remains crowded with endless entertainment, like skywheels, serving as a reminder of our proximity to glamorous Los Angeles—the "City of Angels," the cultural heart of the American West. At Griffith Observatory, I marveled at the breathtaking cityscape, the iconic Hollywood sign, and the ethereal sky. Hollywood Boulevard offered a star-studded walk, paying homage to legendary actors and actresses. The Getty Center proved an art enthusiast's delight, housing an extensive collection of masterpieces. Across the sea from LA, Channel Islands emerged as a vacation paradise, an oasis of natural beauty. From the island's peak, I admired the majestic ocean, rugged cliffs, blossoming flowers, and distant cityscape, forming an enchanting tableau. Further south, San Diego, renowned for its navy harbor, revealed Balboa Park's cultural treasures: museums, gardens, and captivating performances. The USS Midway, a retired aircraft carrier turned museum, provided an enthralling glimpse into naval history.

Texas

Houston, the largest city in Texas, is the city I'm most familiar with due to the great basketball player Yao Ming. At the heart of Houston lies the Water Wall Park, an architectural masterpiece that never fails to impress. The sight of its majestic cascading waterfall, surrounded by lush greenery, creates a tranquil oasis amidst the urban landscape. Continuing my exploration, the Johnson Space Center proved to be a dream come true for a space enthusiast like me. The center's hallowed ground is steeped in the awe-inspiring history of space exploration and NASA's remarkable achievements. The sight of the colossal Saturn V rocket and space shuttle ignited my childhood fascination with the cosmos, filling me with exhilaration and wonder. Houston's museums offered yet another delightful surprise. The exhibits in the Houston Museum of Natural Science, showcasing exquisite crystals, pearls, and gems, left me in awe of nature's beauty and power. The shimmering lights and vibrant colors painted a mesmerizing picture, captivating my senses.

San Antonino, my favorite city in Texas, captivates me with its exquisite blend of nature and history. The enchanting San Antonio River, with its gentle flow mirroring the tranquility of the surroundings, offers me boundless joy as I wander along its picturesque banks. The sprawling green landscape that embraces the river further enriches the serene ambiance, providing an idyllic escape from the bustling city life. The city's historic gem, The Alamo, stands proudly as a testament to its rich heritage of courage and resilience. As I step foot onto this hallowed ground, I am transported back in time, envisioning the significant historical events that unfolded within its ancient walls. The Alamo's imposing presence serves as a poignant reminder of the past and a symbol of the city's enduring spirit.

Austin is the capital of Texas, left me astonished with its rapid transformation into a modern industrial hub. As I explored the city, I couldn't help but be mesmerized by the awe-inspiring Tesla Giga Factory, marveling at its cutting-edge innovations and advancements in technology. Seeking more adventures, I headed to a shooting range, where I embraced the exhilarating experience of firing AR rifles. It was an adrenaline-pumping activity that allowed me to immerse myself in the spirit of Texas's frontier heritage.Amidst the urban hustle, I found solace at the enchanting McKinney Falls State Park. Covered with lush green grass, peculiar stones, and meandering streams, the park exuded a tranquil atmosphere that contrasted beautifully with the city's vibrant energy.

Dallas, the city where my VEX Robot journey began, holds a cherished spot in my heart. Returning after years, I am filled with nostalgia as I witness enthusiastic students diligently preparing for the world championship. Memories come flooding back, evoking a sense of joy and accomplishment. Apart from the sentimental value, Dallas also exudes a laid-back vibe with a plethora of entertainment options. My taste buds rejoiced as I indulged in the local Texas BBQ and flavorful Tacos, immersing myself in the city's culinary delights. The home court of Dallas Mavertick and the intriguing zoo also provided me with relaxing and enjoyable experiences.

The Grand Circle

Page is always the first place that comes to mind when I think of road trips. Located in the center of the grand circle, its breathtaking landscapes include the iconic Horseshoe Bend, a magnificent meander of the Colorado River. Standing on the steep cliffs, the sight of the river carving its way into a perfect horseshoe shape leaves visitors in awe. Not far away lies Antelope Canyon, one of the world's most renowned slot canyons. Its narrow passages, formed by water and wind over millennia, create mesmerizing light beams that dance on the smooth sandstone walls. Exploring this dark and mysterious underground realm feels like stepping into a magical painting. Then, there's Monument Valley, situated on the border of Arizona and Utah. This extraordinary place showcases towering red sandstone mesas and buttes, each one resembling a majestic monument sculpted by nature's hand over eons. Gazing across the vast expanse, you can't help but feel a sense of grandeur and wonder, as if transported into a classic scene from the American West.

The Grand Canyon in Arizona is the only visible canyon from the space. Having visited both the south rim and north rim, I was spellbound by its immense size and stunning vistas. Standing along the rim, I felt an inexplicable connection with the past, as if two individuals from different epochs were gazing at each other across time. What struck me most was the uniformity of the peaks, all magically appearing to have the same height, giving an illusion of the ground rising majestically. This perfect harmony added an unparalleled sense of order and magnificence to the scenery. The Grand Canyon is a testament to the extraordinary power of geological processes and stands as a timeless testament to the majesty of creation.

Utah is a realm of national parks that have etched deep impressions in my heart, each in its own extraordinary way. The first time I laid eyes on the majestic cliffs of Zion, I felt as though I had stepped into a living masterpiece. Whether wandering along the narrows or ascending to Angel's Landing, every moment offered a refreshing communion with nature's grandeur. Bryce Canyon, a fairy-tale realm, takes on an enchanting allure during winter, when a pristine blanket of snow adorns the ground and hoodoos are adorned with delicate icicles. As the sun ascends the horizon, its golden rays breathe life into the hoodoos, igniting a dazzling symphony of colors that seems straight out of a dream. Capital Reef humbles me with its intricate layers of geological history, a testament to the mighty forces that have shaped our planet over eons, as exemplified by the imposing Waterpocket Fold. Canyonlands, with its vast expanse and colossal "monster's footprint," beckons me to embark on a boundless adventure, immersed in the untamed beauty and tranquility of nature. Lastly, Arches National Park unfolds like an artistic dreamscape, where surreal stone arches like the Delicate Arch, the Arches Windows, and the Balance Rock seem like masterpieces delicately crafted by the hands of time. They continue to inspire awe and wonder, reminding me of the ingenuity and charm that Mother Nature has bestowed upon our world.
